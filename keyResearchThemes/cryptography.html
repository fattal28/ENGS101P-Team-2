Cryptography

What is cryptography?

The literal meaning of cryptography is just “secret writing”. [1] It is a science about securing messages transferred in communications and making sure they won’t fall into the wrong hands.
The way to do this is to use encryption, which is the process of converting original human-readable text (plaintext) into unintelligible text that is meaningless without decryption (ciphertext). The process of encryption and decryption is done using a “key”, which is usually a string that is only known to the sender and recipient. A series of mathematical operations are applied to the original text using the key, hence even if the message is intercepted during transmission, it wouldn’t mean anything useful to the third party as they don’t have the key.
