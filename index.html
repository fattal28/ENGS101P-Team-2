Introduction

As the world grows increasingly digital, and more and more of our personal data is stored online, threats to information security are becoming progressively more dangerous. Information Security, or InfoSec for short, exists “to protect the confidentiality, integrity and availability of information assets, whether in storage, processing, or transmission. It is achieved via the application of policy, education, training and awareness, and technology.”[1] This website aims to inform you of the history of the field, a few of the prominent research areas, and current progressions in developing field of information security.

Impacts

Information Security has dramatically influenced the way governments, business organisations and individuals use technology. Without it, the use of digital data would become extremely risky.
In the age of the Internet, most of our confidential information is digitised and stored on electronic devices; and the exchange of information can happen millions of times in a second, for instance, when an online purchase is made. However, this data can always be stolen and used for personal greed. Credit card information might be taken and used elsewhere, company’s financial reports and future plans might be intercepted and sold to competitors - hackers can even gain control of the government’s internal systems and lead to further cyber terrorism activities.
InfoSec is vital to fight against this kind of theft. It prevents unauthorized access to confidential information, encrypts transmitted data, and scans for any malware or security risks. Large organizations typically employ specialists who oversee the security systems, since there is always a huge amount of sensitive information that needs to be managed. [2] Due to rapid advances in technology, InfoSec will play an increasingly critical role and will definitely have greater impacts on governments, organisations and individuals.
