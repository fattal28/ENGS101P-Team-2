History Of Information Security

50 B.C.

Caesar cipher, which is one of the very first encryption methods, was invented in order to protect the confidentiality of important military documents.[1]

1653

With the expansion of postal services, governments started creating official organizations to intercept, decipher, read and reseal letters. For example, the UK Secret Office and Deciphering Branch was established in 1653

1919

During the First World War, code making and decipher sections were greatly used in diplomatic and military headquarters. This resulted in the creation of the Government Code and Cypher School in UK.

1960s

With the development of computer technology, computers were used heavily in processing sensitive data. In order to secure the terminals, many organizations started using passwords and multiple layers of security protection on their devices.

1970s

Before the establishment of massive global networks, organizations used telephone lines to link their devices. Some people started to seek ways to infiltrate phones lines so that they could steal data from connected computers, which made them the first group of hackers.

1980ss

The hazard of hacking became severe against limited security system. In 1982 and 1983, a group of teenagers from Milwaukee, known as "the 414s," broke into over 60 high-profile computers including military and corporate systems. Even though they were seen as harmless pranksters by most people, some experts realized that same techniques could easily be used to cause some real harm.

Now

With the rapid growth in telecommunications and computer software and hardware, information security becomes highly important as the best way to stop cybercrime. Research done in this area is greatly valued by governments and organizations, and have significant impacts on the whole of society.
